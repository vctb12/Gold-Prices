Why Fake Gold Is a Real Problem

Why Fake Gold Is a Real Problem matters because gold decisions combine metal value, local market practice, and personal purpose. In the GCC, the same gram can be viewed as jewellery, savings, a wedding purchase, or an investment position. Start by separating the spot-linked metal value from retail costs such as making charges, VAT, delivery, storage, and dealer spread.

Use live reference data as a baseline, but do not confuse it with a guaranteed shop price. A serious buyer checks the timestamp, source, karat purity, weight, and whether the quote is for buying from a shop or selling back to a dealer. This is especially important in Dubai, Riyadh, Kuwait City, Doha, Manama, Muscat, and other active gold markets where competition is strong but quote formats differ.

Practical due diligence is simple: ask for the calculation in writing, confirm the hallmark, compare at least two providers, and keep invoices. If the item is jewellery, ask how much of the price is metal and how much is craftsmanship. If it is bullion, focus on assay certificate, serial number, premium over spot, and resale policy.
